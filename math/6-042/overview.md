I enrolled 6.042 Mathematics for Computer Science freshman spring after a blizzard. I would walk to lecture in an icy valley formed by snow on either edge of the sidewalk. I stood on mounds of ice to let oncoming students pass, as if we encoutered on an Everest trail rather than a city walkway. This path would close soon for rennovations to the adjacent MET Warehouse (congrats, School of Architecture). The construction continued well after my graduation, and I can't recall what the corner of Mass Ave and Vassar was without the metal scaffolding and orange lattice trees. I do remember, however, performing terribly throughout the term. There are many reasons why so. It was a pity I passed.

I am here to pick up some of the pieces for my younger self, particularly in regards to graph theory. I was always more interested in graphs than the other topics covered in lecture.
